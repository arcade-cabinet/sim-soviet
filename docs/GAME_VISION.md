# Game Vision

**SimSoviet 1917** is a Soviet bureaucrat survival sim about one settlement living through the Soviet century.

The player is the **predsedatel**, not an omnipotent city planner. The settlement grows through autonomous agents. The player survives by reading pressure, nudging priorities, responding to state demands, deciding what to falsify, and choosing who pays the cost when the plan fails.

## 1.0 Promise

Deliver a polished historical campaign from **1917 through 1991**:

1. Revolution and civil war aftermath.
2. Collectivization and coercive agriculture.
3. Industrialization and terror.
4. Great Patriotic War pressure.
5. Reconstruction.
6. Thaw and freeze.
7. Stagnation, reform attempts, and dissolution.

At 1991 the campaign completes. The player sees the campaign summary and may continue the **same settlement** in grounded free play.

## Post-1991 Free Play

Free play is local and conservative:

- no new eras;
- no new settlements;
- no space unlocks;
- no alternate-history global expansion;
- same quotas, aging people, resource pressure, weather, disasters, corruption, and decay.

## Tone

The system is the antagonist. Comedy comes from bureaucracy, contradiction, fear, scarcity, and institutional absurdity, not from fantasy escalation.

## Non-Goals

1.0 does not include deep future progression, Kardashev stages, multi-settlement management, celestial rendering, or post-scarcity crisis domains.
