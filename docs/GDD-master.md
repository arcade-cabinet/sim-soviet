# SimSoviet 1917 GDD

## Core Loop

1. The settlement produces food, materials, power, vodka, labor, and political paperwork.
2. Moscow issues quotas, building mandates, inspections, and political demands.
3. Agents decide local construction, staffing, consumption, maintenance, and survival behavior.
4. Pressure accumulates across classical domains: food, morale, loyalty, housing, political, power, infrastructure, demographic, health, and economic.
5. Historical crises and emergent local crises disrupt the plan.
6. The player responds through broad priorities, reports, political choices, and emergency overrides.
7. The settlement survives, stagnates, or collapses.

## Campaign Structure

| Era | Years | Design Focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Revolution | 1917-1921 | Scarcity, legitimacy, improvised authority |
| Collectivization | 1922-1931 | Grain pressure, coercion, rural disruption |
| Industrialization | 1932-1940 | Factory mandates, terror, unsafe expansion |
| Great Patriotic War | 1941-1944 | Conscription, destruction, evacuation |
| Reconstruction | 1945-1955 | Rebuilding, exhaustion, late Stalinism |
| Thaw And Freeze | 1956-1981 | Reform cycles, space-race prestige, renewed crackdowns |
| Stagnation And Dissolution | 1982-1991 | Corruption, decay, reform failure, campaign completion |

## Victory And Continuation

Reaching 1991 completes the historical campaign and records the score. The player can continue with the same settlement after the summary. Post-campaign free play does not unlock future eras or expansion systems; it keeps local management pressure alive.

## Player Control

The player does not freely place buildings. Mandates and autonomous demand decide what is needed. The player influences priorities, responds to events, and uses limited intervention tools.

## Removed 1.0 Scope

Deep-future escalation, space settlement, post-scarcity economics, multi-settlement relocation, and alternate global timelines are not part of this GDD.
