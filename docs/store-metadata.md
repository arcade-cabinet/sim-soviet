# SimSoviet 1917 — Store Metadata

Copy for app store listings (Apple App Store, Google Play, itch.io, Steam).
Tone: dark-absurdist bureaucratic, matching in-game voice. No earnest
marketing language; no "Manage X! Conquer Y!" shouting.

## Title

**SimSoviet 1917**

## Short description (80 chars max)

A Soviet bureaucrat survival sim. 1917–1991. You cannot be promoted.

*(75 chars)*

## Long description (4,000 chars max)

You are the predsedatel of one settlement in the Union of Soviet Socialist
Republics. It is October 1917. The collective will build itself as families
arrive. Your job is not to place stones.

Moscow sends quotas. Moscow sends reports. Moscow sends the occasional
visitor whose coat is a little too warm for the weather. You will respond
on time, in full, with the correct number of copies, or you will respond
later, in a different tone, from a different room.

There is no skill tree. There is no zone-painting. There is a ledger, and
the ledger always balances — against someone.

**What you actually do:**
- Read reports.
- Approve or refuse directives.
- Decide who delivers the shortfall.
- Sign forms you did not write.
- Watch seventy-four years of Soviet history grind through your settlement.

**What the settlement does:**
- Arrives as a caravan. Builds itself as housing demand builds it.
- Produces food unless the winter, the quota, or the new agricultural
  theory prevents it.
- Occasionally reports you to the district committee. Occasionally doesn't.

**Eras, in order, without fanfare:**
- **Revolution** (1917–1922). Everything is new. This will not last.
- **Collectivization** (1922–1932). The land becomes collective. So do
  the people who used to own it.
- **Industrialization** (1932–1941). Steel above all. Bread, below.
- **Great Patriotic War** (1941–1945). Construction suspended. Everything
  else accelerated.
- **Reconstruction** (1945–1956). Rebuild. The Chairman is still watching.
- **Thaw and Freeze** (1956–1982). The Chairman is not watching. Or is
  watching differently. It is difficult to tell.
- **Stagnation** (1982–1991). The Union will dissolve in August. You are
  not told this in advance.

**December 1991: the end of a campaign.** When the Union dissolves, the
scored campaign closes and the personnel file is finalized. Continue
running the same settlement in post-Soviet free play, or close the file.
Either way, you were here. The archive will remember.

**Not a city builder.** Not a strategy game about winning. Not a
power-fantasy about leading the Soviet Union to victory. A survival sim
about small competence inside large machinery, and the moral cost of
continuing to fill out the forms correctly.
